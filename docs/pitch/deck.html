§ 04 · why this isn't a US tool
05 / 12
indian specificity

The same clause is standard in New York
and void in New Delhi.

A model trained on US contracts learns the wrong priors for Indian users. Three examples where the direction of risk inverts across jurisdictions.

§ 27 · Indian Contract Act 1872
Non-compete, post-employment
The foundational rule in Indian contract law: any restraint on trade or employment is void to that extent, except narrowly on the sale of goodwill. Entire rubric inverts.
USEnforceable in most states with reasonable scope.
IndiaVoid. The clause does not exist, legally.
§ 106 · Transfer of Property Act 1882
Statutory eviction notice
Month-to-month tenancies require 15 days' written notice; year-to-year require 6 months. Not contractual — statutory. Cannot be waived.
USNotice waivers often enforceable; state-by-state.
IndiaWaiver void; landlord must go through Rent Controller.
§ 6 · EPF Act 1952  ·  § 39 · ESI Act 1948
Mandatory social security
12% EPF + 3.25% ESIC are employer-side statutory contributions. No Indian employer may contract out of this for an eligible employee.
USNo direct analog; benefits at employer discretion.
India"CTC inclusive" waivers explicitly illegal.

§ 09 · engineering choices
10 / 12
three non-obvious decisions

Built for the live demo, not the benchmark.

i

LoRA, not full fine-tune.

At rank 8, 99.72% of the encoder is frozen. The pretrained Indian-legal knowledge is structurally preserved — there is no path to catastrophic forgetting. Adapter weights are 1.2 MB; the full model is 510 MB.

Trains 10× faster, ships in a 1.2 MB adapter.
ii

Parallel Claude, not a mega-call.

A single Claude call that analyses thirty clauses takes 60-plus seconds sequentially. N parallel calls via asyncio.gather finish in ten. Failure is isolated — one bad clause doesn't kill the run. The UI fills in progressively.

6× faster wall latency, isolated failure modes.
iii

RAG over clause embeddings.

We already compute InLegalBERT embeddings for similarity scoring. For Q&A we embed the query in the same space, cosine-sim against clause embeddings, retrieve top-three, pass to Claude. No vector DB. Ten lines of code.

Chat stays grounded even on long contracts.

§ 10 · judging criteria
11 / 12
every criterion addressed

Each rubric line points to a specific feature.

Accuracy of clause risk identification
Three independent signals fused: ML risk head (LoRA-adapted InLegalBERT, 3-class), cosine similarity against statute-grounded standard-clause anchors, and rule-based law engine. Composite risk is not a single model's guess — it's triangulated.
Quality of plain-language rewrites
Claude Sonnet with statute-grounded per-clause prompts. Each prompt injects clause type, risk level, and applicable statute. No free-floating generation; every rewrite is tied to a specific §.
Handling of complex nested legal language
Claude-based clause segmentation, not regex. Indian contracts with WHEREAS preambles, nested sub-clauses (1.2.a.iii), and run-on paragraphs are segmented reliably in one LLM call — an architectural choice directly targeting this criterion.
Indian legal context awareness
End-to-end Indian anchoring: InLegalBERT (pretrained on 5.4M Indian docs) · our 6,400-clause Indian synthetic corpus · law_engine.py with hand-verified Indian statute citations · jurisdiction_flag detection on every clause · RAG answers grounded in Indian statute references.
